SCARF

Using the SCARF model to improve cooperation in development teams

Oliver Zeigermann / @DJCordhose

approach (reward) / avoid (threat) response

  • Social behavior is driven by desire to minimize threat and maximize reward
  • Social matters are most important to us
    • in the brains default mode we mainly think about others and our relation to them
    • Social pain feels similar to physical pain - aspirin can reduce social pain
    • Aspirin can reduce social pain
    • Social fear can be as bad as fear of death

SCARF

  • SCARF is a shorthand for a model of triggers that can generate approach or avoid responses
    • S: Status
    • C: Certainty
    • A: Autonomy
    • R: Relatedness
    • F: Fairness
  • Backed by and based on neuroscientistic findings
  • Basic paper: http://www.your-brain-at-work.com/files/NLJ_SCARFUS.pdf

SCARF: S for Status

  • relative importance to others
  • negative example scenario
    • colleague puts you down in front of your boss and other colleagues
    • blaming you of having failed to accomplish some task
  • positive example scenario
    • boss praises you for something you have done

SCARF: C for Certainty

  • being able to predict the future
  • negative example scenario
    • a talk in front of an unknown audience
    • What will people be like? Will they like you? What will the location be like?
  • positive example scenario
    • check the location first
    • rehearse your talk in a secure environment

SCARF: A for Autonomy

  • a sense of control
  • negative example scenario
    • you have given a detailed description of your talk
    • Will you be able to get through the talk in the time you have?
    • now you feel a lack of control
  • positive example scenario
    • give a ver high level description of your talk

SCARF: R for Relatedness

  • safety with others
  • negative example scenario
    • being excluded socially
    • causes you physical pain
    • might make you aggressive
  • positive example scenario
    • being included socially

SCARF: F for Fairness

  • fair exchanges between people
  • extremely high response and probably hardest to control
  • negative example scenario
    • some example as the first, being put down by a colleague
    • his action is just not fair
  • positive example scenario
    • someone of your team gives you fair credit

Default reactions to threats

  • If uncontrolled, severe threats can cause default actions
  • Depending on the particular person those actions can either be
    • fight / attack
    • flee
    • freeze
  • Severly limits your cognitive resources
  • Learning from those situations while in fear is virtually impossible

How to actively improve social interactions

  • Try avoiding actions that triggering avoid responses
  • Try expanding actions that trigger reward responses
  • Raising awareness of social models and their importance

Dealing with social threats

If other people trigger threat responses in you, try...

  • Stating
    • Notice and speak out (silently) what your feeling is
    • E.g. "That makes me angry because it is unfair"
    • Will put you back in control
    • Works best if you can tell other people
    • Sometimes not powerful enough
  • Reappraisal
    • Find another interpretation for the situation
    • E.g. "He did not mean to be rude, he is just stressed out"
    • Much harder, requires much more brain power
    • Much more efficient

Prerequisite: Mindfulness

  • All these actions require almost permanent attention
  • Without noticing you have been threatened you can do nothing against it
  • You only have a very short time span until automated responses kick it
  • Mindfulness means to be intentionally in a situation without judgement, just observing
  • Being in the situation gives you the permanent attention
  • Can become a permanent mode if practiced over and over again
  • Science also tells us that this even makes us healthier and happier
  • Dan Siegel on mindfulness

Final disclaimer

  • SCARF is just a model
  • It is not the reality
  • SCARF models a certain aspect of reality only
  • the SCARF model is not fully accurate for the aspect either
  • model are still a good means of reasoning on and discussing such an aspect
  • Know anything better?
  • Know other models?
